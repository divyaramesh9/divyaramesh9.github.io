My Research

Exploring AI applications in mental health and cultural competency in therapeutic settings.

Columbia Spoken Language Processing Group

Research Assistant

I am currently a Research Assistant under Ziwei Gong and Prof. Julia Hirschberg at the Columbia Spoken Language Processing Group.

Lab Focus

Our lab is working to develop AI-powered dialogue agents using LLMs to provide accessible and democratized mental health support for individuals with limited resources, with a focus on building models that incorporate continual learning capabilities to adapt to long dialogue contexts.

Current Research

Currently, I am investigating the efficacy of LLMs in multicultural, therapeutic settings, specifically evaluating cultural competency in clinical contexts. I am assessing existing use cases of LLMs across diverse cultural environments and exploring how model training data composition affects effectiveness in handling culturally specific mental health scenarios. I am developing methods to evaluate and benchmark LLMs for cultural competency, working toward frameworks that can guide model selection for therapeutic applications across different cultural contexts.
